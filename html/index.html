AI and Academic Autonomy Study

May 2025 - Jun 2025

Conducted a statistical study exploring whether undergraduates are losing independent thinking skills due to frequent AI use. Using surveys and applying descriptive, correlation, and regression analysis on responses from 30 NIBM undergraduates, we examined the relationship between AI usage, productivity, and academic confidence. Findings showed AI tools are widely used for coding and research, with a moderate trend suggesting frequent AI users may feel slightly less confident working independently, highlighting the need to balance AI support with critical thinking development in education.
